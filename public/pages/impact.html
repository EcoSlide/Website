IMPACT PILLARS

Three ways EcoSlides creates impact

Environmental, social, and circular economy benefits—built into the product.

Environmental

Repurposing tire rubber helps reduce dumping and burning—two common paths that pollute soil, air, and water.

  • Less tire waste in landfills
  • Lower pollution risk from burning
  • Longer material life through reuse

Social

Simple production and local craftsmanship can create opportunities and promote practical skill-based work.

  • Supports local makers
  • Promotes accessible impact
  • Community-driven production model

Circular Economy

We keep valuable material in use by turning waste into a durable product designed for everyday wear.

  • Waste → resource
  • Design for durability
  • Continuous improvement mindset

MEASUREMENT

How we measure impact

EcoSlides measures impact through clear, realistic indicators aligned with local environmental and production contexts in Panama and Latin America. All metrics are designed to be verifiable, scalable, and appropriate for early-stage prototypes.

Tires repurposed

1-50 tires

In Panama and many LATAM countries, end-of-life tires are frequently burned or improperly disposed of.

Tracking method:

  • Per production batch
  • Logged during material collection

Target: Increase progressively as prototype validation advances.

Rubber Diverted from Waste

Baseline to be measured (kg)

Estimated total weight of tire rubber reused instead of being burned or sent to landfills.

Measurement approach:

  • Average rubber weight per sole
  • Multiplied by number of units produced

Target: Establish first verified value after weighing initial prototypes.

Product Durability

Wear tests cycle

Tracks how long prototypes maintain comfort, structural integrity, and usability under real world conditions.

Method:

  • User wear testing
  • Number of user feedback cycles and visual inspection

Target: Minimum of 10 users completing full wear cycles before design iteration.

Local collaboration

Partners

Number of local artisans, workshops, or suppliers involved in sourcing, testing, or assembly.

Regional relevance:

  • Supports local production models common in Panama and LATAM, emphasizing small-scale manufacturing and skill transfer.

Target: Minimum of 10 local artisans or workshops involved in sourcing, testing, or assembly.

Tip: EcoSlides focuses on achievable impact first prioritizing durability, reuse, and local collaboration before scaling production.
